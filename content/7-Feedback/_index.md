# Sharing and Feedback

During the First Cloud Journey program, I found the learning environment comfortable, and people were willing to help when I had difficulties. The mentor gave clear guidance and usually allowed me to investigate first before pointing out what needed to be corrected.

What satisfied me most was the friendly environment and the mentor's enthusiastic support. Whenever I had difficulties, the mentor was willing to explain the problem and point out what I needed to improve. I also had the opportunity to work on a practical project instead of only learning theory. Through RecruitPro, I learned how to deploy an application on AWS and became familiar with several common configuration issues.

While working with team **NTM**, the members discussed tasks and supported one another. However, task assignment and progress updates were not always consistent, so the team could communicate more frequently.

I think the program could include more shared practice sessions or architecture reviews so teams can learn from one another. Additional content about AWS costs and CI/CD would also be useful for students.

## Reflection

At the beginning of the internship, I was not familiar with the working approach and sometimes did not know where to start when receiving a new topic. I was also hesitant to ask questions before I fully understood the problem. I gradually became more proactive by reading the available material first, noting unclear points, and discussing specific questions with the mentor and my teammates.

In the future, I want to improve my communication, time management, and ability to present a problem clearly. I also hope to participate in more practical projects so I can better understand the working environment and determine my own development direction.

Overall, I was satisfied with the internship. I gained more knowledge about AWS and improved my ability to find documentation and troubleshoot issues. I would like to continue learning about cloud technology and improve the project in the future.

Finally, I would like to thank the mentor, admin team, and other members for supporting me throughout the internship.
